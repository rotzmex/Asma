SERVICES

Education

The Education Program enhances livelihoods and social cohesion through the provision of quality primary education to 3,000 Syrian refugee children, as well as Basic Literacy and Numeracy (BLN) to 700 Syrian, Lebanese and Palestinian youth and adults, especially mothers. Our projects operate across 10 Teaching Centers in mobile shipping caravans located inside tent settlements in Bekaa and Arsal. Our teachers, a majority of which are Syrian, are well trained and committed to improving skills.

Health

In 2013, MAPs launched The Health Program Lebanon (HPL) to improve Syrian refugees’ access to quality and affordable healthcare in the Bekaa Valley. HPL employs 112 healthcare workers providing services across six projects treating about 13,000 patients per month. It reduces the physical and financial barriers to healthcare to help Syrians manage chronic diseases and life-threating illnesses such as diabetes, respiratory infections, and dysentery due to lack of clean water, sanitation and nutrition.

Research

RESEARCH AT MAPS COMMUNITY-BASED RESEARCH FOR CHANGE MAPs’ Research Center is community-based and participatory, bridging gaps between the perspectives of refugees and academic research. The Research Center strengthens local research capacity through research training, workshops, lectures and paid internships for Syrian youth.

ABOUT US

Multi-Aid Programs (MAPs) is a Syrian-led, non-governmental organization registered in Lebanon since 2013. We operate in marginalized communities across Bekaa and Arsal, providing health services and education to Syrian refugees, vulnerable Lebanese groups, and Palestinians. Our 326 volunteers work tirelessly to design and implement innovative programs to improve both the individual and the community at large.
